Understanding Cinema Lighting

Lighting is a key element in cinematography that enhances mood, depth, and storytelling.

ðŸ“Œ Types of Lighting in Film

  • Key Light: The primary light source illuminating the subject.
  • Fill Light: Used to soften shadows and balance the lighting.
  • Backlight: Adds depth by separating the subject from the background.
  • High Key Lighting: Bright and even lighting, often used in comedies and commercials.
  • Low Key Lighting: Dark and moody, often used in horror and thriller films.
  • Natural Lighting: Using available sunlight or practical lights for a realistic look.
